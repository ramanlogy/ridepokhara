Top 10 Scenic Routes for Bike Trips in Pokhara 2024

Pokhara is not just Nepal's adventure capital—it's a motorcyclist's paradise. With stunning Himalayan backdrops, serene lakes, and winding mountain roads, renting a bike in Lakeside Pokhara opens up a world of unforgettable journeys. Whether you're on a Royal Enfield or a nimble scooter, these routes will take your breath away.

Why Rent a Bike in Pokhara?

Pokhara's unique geography makes it perfect for two-wheeled exploration. Located in the lap of the Annapurna range, the city offers everything from smooth lakeside roads to challenging mountain trails. Bike rental in Pokhara Lakeside has become incredibly popular because it gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace—no tour groups, no schedules, just you and the open road.

Most rental shops in Lakeside (Baidam area) offer a variety of bikes from automatic scooters (starting at Rs. 1000/day) to powerful Royal Enfield motorcycles (Rs. 2500-3500/day). The best part? You can pick up your bike early morning and return it by evening, making it perfect for day trips.

Quick Rental Tips

  • License Required: International Driving Permit or valid motorcycle license
  • Deposit: Usually passport copy + Rs. 5000-15000 refundable deposit
  • Insurance: Always ask about coverage—some rentals include basic insurance
  • Fuel: Most bikes are given with full tank, return with full tank
  • Best Season: October-November and March-April for clear mountain views

Route 1: Sarangkot Sunrise Ride

Sarangkot Hill Station

Distance
13 km from Lakeside
Time Required
2-3 hours
Difficulty
Easy (Paved road)
Best Bike
Any scooter or motorcycle

This is THE most popular bike ride from Pokhara for good reason. Leave Lakeside at 5:00 AM and ride up the smooth, winding road to Sarangkot viewpoint (1,600m elevation). The sunrise over Machhapuchhre (Fishtail Mountain) and the Annapurna range is absolutely spectacular.

Route Details: Start from Lakeside → Bindabasini Temple → Pame → Sarangkot. The road is entirely paved and well-maintained. There are several small cafes at the top serving hot tea and breakfast. Even beginners can handle this ride comfortably.

Pro Tip: The temperature at the top can be 10°C colder than Lakeside in winter. Bring a jacket! Also, the road gets crowded with tourist buses after 7 AM, so starting early gives you the best experience.

Route 2: World Peace Pagoda Circuit

Peace Pagoda (Shanti Stupa)

Distance
8 km from Lakeside
Time Required
2 hours
Difficulty
Medium (Steep climb)
Best Bike
Scooter or 150cc+

A scooter rental in Pokhara is perfect for this route. The Peace Pagoda sits on top of a hill overlooking Phewa Lake, offering 360-degree views of the valley and mountains. The road is steep but entirely paved.

Route Options: You can approach from either Chhorepatan (easier, longer) or Damside (steeper, shorter). We recommend the Damside route for the challenge and better views. Park at the base and walk the final 100 steps for an unforgettable panorama.

Route 3: Begnas and Rupa Lake Loop

Twin Lakes Adventure

Distance
30 km loop
Time Required
Half day
Difficulty
Easy
Best Bike
Any bike

Escape the tourist crowds with this peaceful ride to Begnas and Rupa lakes, located 15 km east of Pokhara. These pristine lakes are far less crowded than Phewa Lake but equally beautiful. The road is quiet, smooth, and perfect for a relaxed ride.

Recommended Route: Lakeside → Siswa Chowk → Begnas Bazaar → Begnas Lake → Rupa Lake → Return via same route. Stop at one of the lakeside resorts for fresh fish lunch and enjoy swimming in summer.

Route 4: Kahun Danda Off-Road Adventure

Hidden Viewpoint Challenge

Distance
25 km from Lakeside
Time Required
4-5 hours
Difficulty
Hard (Off-road)
Best Bike
RE Himalayan, Dirt bikes

For experienced riders seeking adventure, Kahun Danda is a challenging off-road climb that rewards you with one of the best viewpoints in the Pokhara valley. The last 8 km is rough dirt track—this is where a Royal Enfield rental in Pokhara really shines, especially the Himalayan 411 model.

Important: This route is NOT recommended for beginners or scooters. You need off-road riding experience and a bike with good ground clearance. Best attempted in dry season (October-March).

Route 5: Bat Cave & Mahendra Cave Loop

Natural Wonders Tour

Distance
12 km round trip
Time Required
3 hours
Difficulty
Easy
Best Bike
Any bike

Head north from Lakeside toward Batulechaur to explore the famous Mahendra Cave and nearby Bat Cave (Chamero Gufa). The ride is short and easy, making it perfect for families or those new to motorcycle rental in Pokhara.

Combine this with a visit to the Butterfly Museum and International Mountain Museum for a full day of educational sightseeing.

Route 6: Naudanda Panorama Ride

360° Mountain Views

Distance
28 km from Lakeside
Time Required
4 hours
Difficulty
Medium
Best Bike
125cc or higher

Naudanda (meaning "nine hills") offers some of the best mountain panoramas accessible by bike from Pokhara. The ride takes you through traditional Gurung villages and terraced farmland.

Route: Lakeside → Kaskikot → Naudanda. The road is paved but narrow in places. Stop at one of the hilltop lodges for lunch with a view. On clear days, you can see Dhaulagiri, Annapurna, and Manaslu ranges simultaneously.

Route 7: Ghorepani-Poon Hill Extended Trip

For the ultimate Himalayan bike tour from Pokhara, the ride to Ghorepani takes 2-3 days and requires serious commitment. This is the route for adventure riders with proper touring bikes (Royal Enfield Classic 350 or Himalayan 411 recommended).

Distance: 180 km round trip | Duration: 2-3 days | Difficulty: Expert level

The road from Nayapul onward is rough and challenging, but the reward is riding through the heart of the Annapurna Conservation Area. You'll need permits, warm gear, and ideally a guide if you're unfamiliar with mountain riding.

Route 8: Hemja and Armala Villages

Experience authentic rural Nepal just 20 minutes from Lakeside. The ride to Hemja village and beyond to Armala offers a glimpse of traditional farming life with the Himalayas as your backdrop.

Perfect for: Photographers, cultural enthusiasts, easy riders. The roads are flat and well-paved. Great for testing out your cheap scooter rental in Pokhara before attempting longer routes.

Route 9: Sedi Bagar & Tal Barahi Temple

A short urban ride that's perfect for evenings. Cruise along the Lakeside road from Baidam to Sedi Bagar, stopping at Tal Barahi Temple (accessible by boat from the shore). The lakeside promenade is especially beautiful at sunset.

Best Time: Late afternoon/evening. Combine with dinner at one of the lakeside restaurants. This is a beginner-friendly route with no traffic or hills.

Route 10: Upper Mustang Expedition (Multi-Day)

The ultimate bucket-list ride for serious motorcyclists. Upper Mustang is a restricted area that requires special permits and a registered guide. The 10-14 day journey takes you through arid, Tibet-like landscapes to the ancient walled city of Lo Manthang.

Requirements:

  • Restricted Area Permit (USD 500 for 10 days)
  • Royal Enfield Himalayan or similar adventure bike
  • Off-road riding experience essential
  • Best season: May-October (road closed in winter)
  • Support vehicle recommended for luggage

Most bike rental shops in Pokhara Lakeside can arrange complete Mustang packages including bike, guide, permits, and accommodation. Budget approximately Rs. 150,000-200,000 for the full experience.

Safety Tips for Biking in Pokhara

  • Always wear a helmet: It's legally required and can save your life on mountain roads
  • Check your bike: Test brakes, lights, horn, and tire pressure before leaving the rental shop
  • Carry essentials: Water, basic first aid kit, phone with local SIM card, map/GPS
  • Inform someone: Tell your hotel or a friend which route you're taking
  • Watch for animals: Cows, goats, and dogs often wander on roads—be alert
  • Drive on the left: Nepal follows left-hand traffic rules
  • Take it slow: Mountain roads have blind corners—speed limits exist for a reason

Best Bikes for Each Route Type

For Easy Paved Routes (Sarangkot, Peace Pagoda, Begnas): Honda Dio/Activa automatic scooters are perfect. They're economical (50+ km per liter), easy to maneuver, and available from Rs. 1000-1200/day.

For Medium Distance Touring (Naudanda, Hemja, Bat Caves): Yamaha FZ, Pulsar 150, or Royal Enfield Classic 350 offer more power and comfort. Rental cost: Rs. 1800-2500/day.

For Off-Road & Mountain Adventures (Kahun Danda, Mustang, Ghorepani): Royal Enfield Himalayan 411 is the gold standard. Purpose-built for the Himalayas with high ground clearance, long-range fuel tank, and comfortable ergonomics. Rental: Rs. 3500-4000/day.

When to Visit & Ride in Pokhara

Best Seasons:

  • Autumn (September-November): Crystal clear mountain views, perfect weather, festivals season
  • Spring (March-May): Warm weather, rhododendron blooms, good visibility
  • Winter (December-February): Clear views but cold mornings, less crowded
  • Monsoon (June-August): Not recommended—heavy rain, landslides, poor visibility
